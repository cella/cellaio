Cella M. Sum is an interdisciplinary researcher and PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University's Human-Computer Interaction Institute.

Her research focuses on AI, labor, disability, and the future of care work. Drawing from postcolonial, feminist, and critical disability perspectives, she examines the politics of care in technologies. Using participatory design methods, she works with community stakeholders to co-create more just alternatives.

Cella holds a Masters in Human-Computer Interaction and Design from University of California Irvine and a Bachelors in Computer Science from Shippensburg University. She has over 15 years of industry experience in software engineering, UX research, and design.

Cella Monet Sum is a design researcher and PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University's Human-Computer Interaction Institute.

Her research focuses on AI, labor, disability, and the future of care work. Drawing from postcolonial, feminist, and critical disability studies, she examines the politics of care in technologies. Using participatory design methods, she works with community stakeholders to co-create more just alternatives.

Cella holds a Masters in Human-Computer Interaction and Design from University of California Irvine and a Bachelors in Computer Science from Shippensburg University.
